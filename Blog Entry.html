What I have learned

Computing means using computers to solve problems and do tasks. It could be simple things like using a calculator or more complicated tasks like making new ways for computers to learn. Computers deal with different kinds of information, like numbers, words, and pictures. They help store, find, organize, and understand this information. Computers can even create new stuff, like writing or images.

The word "computer" started with people doing math, then it moved to machines like calculators, and now we have electronic devices. Today's computers take in information, work with it, show the results, and keep what's important. Computing is about solving problems, handling information, and using technology to make things better in lots of different areas.

My Favorite Lessons

Learning about computer security was really interesting. It showed how to keep important information safe and what to do in different situations. The scenarios they shared helped me understand how to handle data better. They made me think about how to protect sensitive information by using things like encryption and classifying data properly. It also taught me about different levels of information, like "Confidential" and "Restricted." This lesson helped me see why it's so important to keep data safe, both at work and in everyday life.

Creating my website was like a big adventure. It was really cool to design and make it look awesome, but sometimes, it got really hard. Figuring out all the technical stuff was tough and made me feel a bit stressed. Even when it got tricky, though, it felt amazing to see it all coming together in the end.
